Trail map meets tide chart

Infrastructure for someone who climbs, surfs, trail runs, and treats the gym and nutrition as serious support for that life. The visual language borrows from technical outdoor gear and honest ledgers — not fitness apps. Dark, warm, dense enough to be useful, calm enough to leave quickly.

01 — Design posture

Three principles, made visible

The design must make these visible without narrating them.

Fidelity is visible

A weighed meal and a photo guess must look different on screen. Confidence is a visual property, not metadata.

Silence is legible

When the app says nothing, the UI makes clear that nothing crossed the threshold — not that something broke.

Tiers are felt

Logged facts, accumulated trends, and modeled scores occupy distinct registers. Tier-3 never dominates tier-1.

06 — Fidelity visualization

Confidence is a visual property

The product's distinctive design problem. A weighed meal and a photo guess must read differently at a glance — encoded by opacity, stroke style, and dot style together, never opacity alone.

High · ≥0.8
Full opacity, solid stroke, solid filled dots.
Barcode scan · scale
Mid · 0.4–0.8
0.7 opacity, solid stroke, hollow ring dots.
Text entry · recipe
Low · <0.4
0.45 opacity, dashed stroke, dotted / no dot.
Photo guess · AI estimate

The segmented bar (top-right of each card) is the always-on indicator on food-log entries and any AI-estimated value — sandstone for filled segments, border for empty.

10 — Do & Don't

Spine rules

Do
Show fidelity visually on every data point that has it.
Let the trend line be the biggest element on a data screen.
Use slate to visually demote tier-3 modeled values.
Use the display font for section titles — uppercase, tracked.
Use tabular mono for any number compared vertically.
Don't
Use bright or saturated accents. Nothing in the palette screams.
Use green/red for ± — use olive and muted warm red.
Put tier-3 data at the same size as tier-1.
Add border-radius to chart containers or data panels.
Use the display font below section-header level.
